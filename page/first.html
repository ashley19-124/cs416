The Microsoft Corporation

Sources: 1 2 3

Founded in 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen in 1975, Microsoft was created to develop and sell BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800, a microcomputer created in 1974. Currently, it is multinational corporation that is ubiquitous in the tech world. It is known for its variety of iconic products from software to hardware, including the Windows OS, Microsoft Office Suite, Edge web browser, Xbox, and much more.

From its initial public offering (IPO) in 1986, Microsoft's share price has increased from just $0.06 to almost $360 in April 2025. However, this journey was in no way smooth; from scandals with the US government to poor business decisions, Microsoft has had its fair share of difficulty. Despite that, it has remained a staple of the modern tech scene and will continue to be for years to come.

The Founders and Their Impacts

Bill Gates   Paul Allen
 
After co-founding the company in 1975, Gates served as its CEO for the first 25 years. During his time as CEO, he also became president and chairman of the board when the company was incorporated in 1981. After stepping down as CEO, he took on the role of chief software architect and stayed in it until 2008. He then stepped down as chairman of the board in 2014 and completely resigned from the board in 2020. Currently, he serves as a technology adviser to the current CEO and other high-ranking leaders at Microsoft.   Paul Allen is credited with creating the name "Microsoft", which comes from the combination of the words "microcomputer" and "software". When Gates became Microsoft's president and chairman of the board in 1981, Allen became the executive vice president and vice chairman. Allen left Microsoft in 1982 after being diagonsed with Hodgkin's lymphoma but remained on the board of directors until 2000. He passed away in 2018 due to complications with cancer.
